Dark Waters

Indie Film · Post Production · Foley

Foley is the art of making the world believable one footstep at a time. Dark Waters is a 14-minute psychological thriller set almost entirely in a flooded basement — a film where sound design is the primary instrument of dread.

Every sonic event in the film was designed from scratch: the particular hollow of boots on submerged concrete, the difference between water dripping on plastic versus rusted metal, the wet weight of a waterlogged jacket being pulled from a body. We recorded across 12 foley sessions, often in a rented warehouse with a shallow water tank.

The most technically demanding sequence: a 90-second chase where the protagonist splashes through knee-high water in absolute darkness. We layered six separate water recordings at different pitches, panning them in a circle around the listener's head to simulate spatial disorientation. Festival audiences consistently cite it as the most frightening sequence in the film.
