Still Frequencies

Short Film · Post Production · Audio Mix

Still Frequencies is a 22-minute documentary about urban silence — the paradox of finding quiet in the noisiest cities on earth. The film's audio design had to embody its subject: present, textured, but never overwhelming. Every mix decision was a philosophical one.

The production sound was recorded in challenging environments: traffic-adjacent interviews, echoing concrete spaces, and a rooftop in a typhoon. iZotope RX 10 was the first pass — de-reverbing, de-noising, and restoring clarity to voices that had been swallowed by the city.

The final mix was delivered at broadcast standard (-23 LUFS integrated, -1 dBTP true peak) with a separate music-and-effects stem for international sales. The documentary went on to screen at three festivals and win Best Sound Design at an independent film competition.
